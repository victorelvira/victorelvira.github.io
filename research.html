Research interests

My research interests are mostly in Monte Carlo methods for Bayesian inference. In particular, I research in the batch problem (all data are available from the beginning) and the problem where the data arrive sequentially.

Batch Bayesian inference

My research is mostly focused in importance sampling methods where several proposal densities are available (multiple importance sampling) and where the proposals are adapted (adaptive importance sampling).
  • V. Elvira, L. Martino, D. Luengo, and M. Bugallo, "Generalized Multiple Importance Sampling", submitted, 2015. arXiv PDF Version 
  • L. Martino, V. Elvira, D. Luengo, and J. Corander, "Layered Adaptive Importance Sampling", Statistics and Computing, Vol. 27, No. 3, pp. 599-623, May. 2017.
  • V. Elvira, L. Martino, D. Luengo, and M. Bugallo, "Improving Population Monte Carlo: Alternative Weighting and Resampling Schemes", Signal Processing, Vol. 131, pp. 77-91, February, 2017. arXiv PDF Version 
  • L. Martino, V. Elvira, and F. Louzada, "Effective Sample Size for Importance Sampling Based on the Discrepancy Measures", Signal Processing, Vol. 131, pp. 386-401, February, 2017. arXiv
  • V. Elvira, L. Martino, D. Luengo, and M. Bugallo, "Heretical Mutiple Importance Sampling" IEEE Signal Processing Letters, vol. 23, no. 10, pp. 1474-1478, October, 2016.
  • V. Elvira, L. Martino, D. Luengo, and M. Bugallo, "Efficient Multiple Importance Sampling Estimators", IEEE Signal Processing Letters, vol. 22, no. 10, pp. 1757-1761, March, 2015.
  • V. Elvira, L. Martino, D. Luengo, J. Corander, "A Gradient Adaptive Population Importance Sampler", IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2015), Brisbane, Australia, April, 2015.
I am also interested in Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods:
  • L. Martino, V. Elvira, D. Luengo, and J. Corander, and F. Louzada "Orthogonal parallel MCMC methods for sampling and optimization", Digital Signal Processing, Vol. 58, pp. 64-84, November, 2016. arXiv
  • L. Martino, V. Elvira, D. Luengo, F. Louzada, "Parallel Metropolis chains with cooperative adaptation", IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2016), Shanghai, China, March, 2016.

Sequential Bayesian inference

I am interested in the problem of assesing the convergence and adapting the number of particles in sequential Monte Carlo methods (particle filtering):
  • V. Elvira, J. Míguez, and P. M. Djuric, "Adapting the Number of Particles in Sequential Monte Carlo Methods through an Online Scheme for Convergence Assessment", IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing, vol. 65, no. 7, pp. 1781-1794, 2017.
  • V. Elvira, J. Miguez, and P. M. Djuric, "A Novel Algorithm for Adapting the Number of Particles in Particle Filtering", IEEE Sensor Array and Multichannel Signal Processing Workshop (SAM 2016), Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, July, 2016.
  • V. Elvira, J. Miguez, and P. M. Djuric, "Online Adaptation of the Number of Particles of Sequential Monte Carlo Methods", IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2016), Shanghai, China, March, 2016.
